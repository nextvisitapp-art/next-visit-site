Privacy Policy

Last updated: 3 June 2026

The short version: Next Visit is a private space for two people. Your photos, memories, and lists are visible only to you and your partner — never public, never sold. We collect the minimum we need to run the app, we don't use tracking cookies or advertising, and you can delete your account and personal data at any time.

This policy explains what information Next Visit ("Next Visit", "we", "us") collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. It covers both of our surfaces:

  • The Next Visit app (web and the iOS/Android app at next-visit.app) — the private space where couples keep memories and plan time together.
  • The Next Visit planner (go.next-visit.app) — a no-login tool for sketching out a trip.

1. Information we collect

In the app

We only collect what you choose to add. Depending on how you use Next Visit, that can include:

  • Account identifiers. When you first open the app we create an anonymous account so your space works without a sign-up form. If you choose to enable account recovery, we also store the email address you provide.
  • Profile details. Your name, and optionally a birthday, contact number, and profile photo.
  • Your couple space. A pairing code used to connect with your partner, your partner's name and birthday (as added by either of you), your relationship details such as an anniversary date, and a shared home city or airport if you set one.
  • Your content. Photos and videos you upload (including any metadata embedded in those files, such as capture date or location), captions, memories, notes, bucket-list and wishlist items, quiz answers, and trip details like destinations, dates, cost estimates, and availability.
  • Notifications. If you turn on notifications, a push token for your device, a badge count, and your notification preferences.
  • Local settings. Preferences such as your light/dark appearance choice are stored on your own device.

In the planner

The planner has no accounts and no login. The trip details you enter (origin, destination, dates, number of travellers, preferences) live in your browser while you use it and are used to generate suggestions and previews. We store only your light/dark preference on your device. If you share a trip link, the trip's basic details (such as destination and dates) are encoded into the link so the preview card can render.

What we don't do

We do not use advertising, tracking pixels, or third-party analytics, and we do not set tracking cookies. The only data stored in your browser is the small set of preferences and sign-in helpers described above.

2. How we use your information

  • To run your private space and sync it between you and your partner.
  • To store and show your photos, memories, lists, and trips.
  • To generate the features you ask for, such as AI date ideas, trip suggestions, and destination photos.
  • To send you notifications you've opted into.
  • To let you recover access to your account by email.
  • To keep the service secure, debug problems, and prevent abuse.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your private content to show you ads.

3. When information leaves Next Visit

To provide certain features we send limited information to trusted service providers. They process it on our behalf or to fulfil your request, and each has its own privacy policy.

  • Hosting & infrastructure. Our apps and backend run on Google Firebase (authentication, database, file storage, and push messaging) and Vercel (web hosting and serverless functions). Your account data, content, and uploaded media are stored with Firebase.
  • AI features. When you request date ideas, quiz insights, or trip suggestions, the relevant inputs (for example a location, your preferences and mood, partner names and quiz answers, or trip origin/destination and dates) are sent to Anthropic to generate a response. These inputs are processed to produce your result.
  • Search & photos. City search and destination imagery use public services including Open-Meteo, OpenStreetMap/Nominatim, Wikipedia, and (in the planner) Pexels. A search term such as a city or activity name is sent to fetch results.
  • Music. If you add a song to a memory, your search query may be sent to music providers such as Apple iTunes Search or Spotify to find the track.
  • Account-recovery email. If you enable recovery, we use an email provider to deliver your secure sign-in link or code.
  • Travel booking partners (planner). The planner links out to third parties such as GetYourGuide, Stay22, Viator, Klook, Skyscanner, and Uber so you can look at or book travel. When you follow these links or use an embedded panel, details such as your destination and dates are passed along, and those companies' own terms and privacy policies apply. Some links are affiliate links.

We may also disclose information if required by law, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users and our service.

4. Storage, security & access

Your content is stored with Firebase and access is scoped to your couple space: only you and your linked partner can read your space's data and media. We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your information. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, so we can't guarantee absolute security.

5. Retention & deletion

We keep your information for as long as your account is active. You can delete your account at any time from You → delete account in the app. When you do:

  • Your personal details — name, email, profile photo, birthday, contact number, and notification tokens — are removed, and your email is released for reuse.
  • Per-user records such as your availability entries and notifications are deleted.
  • Shared content you created together (memories, photos, lists, trips) remains available to your partner so their copy of your shared history stays intact, unless they also delete their account.

If you'd like us to delete your data for you, email support@next-visit.app. Planner trips are never stored on our servers, so there's nothing to delete — closing your browser clears them.

6. Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the GDPR or CCPA), you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing. You can edit most details directly in the app, delete your account as described above, or contact us at support@next-visit.app to make a request. We won't discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

7. Children

Next Visit is intended for adults and is not directed to children. You must be at least 18 years old (or the age of majority where you live) to use the app. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children; if you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we'll remove it.

8. International transfers

Our service providers may process and store information in countries other than your own. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards for those transfers. By using Next Visit you understand your information may be processed in these locations.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes we'll update the "last updated" date above and, where appropriate, let you know in the app.

10. Contact us

Questions about this policy or your data? Email support@next-visit.app.
